Create a 20-point quiz on photosynthesis for a 10th grade Biology class. The quiz should assess the following content: (1) chloroplast structure and function, (2) the light-dependent reactions (inputs, outputs, location), and (3) the Calvin cycle / light-independent reactions (inputs, outputs, location). Also include one question specifically probing the common misconception that plants get their food from the soil rather than from photosynthesis.

Format the quiz with three sections:
- Section 1 — Multiple Choice (8 questions, 1 point each): Questions should target "remember" and "understand" levels of Bloom's Taxonomy. Four answer choices per question.
- Section 2 — Short Answer (2 questions, 3 points each): Questions should target "apply" or "analyze" level. Each question should have a clear expectation of answer length (2–3 sentences).
- Section 3 — Diagram Labeling (1 diagram, 6 points): Provide a blank chloroplast diagram with 6 numbered arrows; students write the correct label for each arrow. Include labels such as thylakoid membrane, stroma, granum, and the locations of each photosynthesis stage.

After the quiz, provide a complete answer key with brief explanations for why each multiple choice distractor is incorrect. Note the point value next to each question on the student-facing version.
